The Science Behind the Screen

Triple Screened — Not Just a Label

Most bedding companies filter once. We screen three times — each pass targeting progressively finer particles. The result is bedding that removes the specific particle sizes (PM2.5–PM10) linked to Inflammatory Airway Disease in racehorses.

Peer-reviewed research published in Equine Veterinary Journal links respirable dust in stalls to reduced VO₂ max, impaired mucus clearance, and measurable performance loss in racehorses. Clean air isn't a luxury — it's a competitive advantage.

Read the Full Science →
Pass 1
Coarse Debris Removal

Eliminates large debris, oversized particles, and foreign material. Ensures uniform pellet/shaving size.

Pass 2
Fine Particle Separation

Targets the mid-range particles that traditional single-screen processes miss. Dramatically reduces visible dust.

Pass 3
Respirable Dust Elimination

Final ultra-fine pass targets PM2.5–PM10 particles — the particles that reach deep into airway tissue and trigger IAD.

Nationwide Truckload Delivery

Same Bedding. Every Track. Every Stop.

Horses that ship between tracks carry their performance history with them — but most operations change bedding every time they change venues. That introduces a variable nobody talks about: different dust levels, different absorbency, different organic content. When a horse is used to one bedding and suddenly it's standing in something else, that stall environment has changed.

We deliver full truckloads of the same triple-screened pine bedding to racetracks across the country. If you're running horses at Churchill, Keeneland, and Saratoga in the same season, they can be on the same product at every stop — consistent dust levels, consistent moisture control, consistent stall environment from the home barn to the test barn.

For test barns and quarantine facilities specifically, using a single, well-documented bedding source eliminates one more potential question. The bedding is the same product, from the same source, every time.
