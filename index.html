The Science Behind the Screen

Triple Screened — Not Just a Label

Most bedding companies filter once. We screen three times — each pass targeting progressively finer particles. The result is bedding that removes the specific particle sizes (PM2.5–PM10) linked to Inflammatory Airway Disease in racehorses.

Peer-reviewed research published in Equine Veterinary Journal links respirable dust in stalls to reduced VO₂ max, impaired mucus clearance, and measurable performance loss in racehorses. Clean air isn't a luxury — it's a competitive advantage.

Read the Full Science →
Pass 1
Coarse Debris Removal

Eliminates large debris, oversized particles, and foreign material. Ensures uniform pellet/shaving size.

Pass 2
Fine Particle Separation

Targets the mid-range particles that traditional single-screen processes miss. Dramatically reduces visible dust.

Pass 3
Respirable Dust Elimination

Final ultra-fine pass targets PM2.5–PM10 particles — the particles that reach deep into airway tissue and trigger IAD.
