Dust, Respiratory Health & IAD

Inflammatory Airway Disease (IAD) is the most common respiratory condition in performance horses. Unlike heaves (RAO), IAD affects younger horses and often shows no outward symptoms — but it causes measurable reductions in VO₂ max, recovery rate, and race-day performance. Studies consistently identify respirable dust (PM2.5–PM10 range) as the primary stall-environment trigger, and bedding is the largest continuous source of that dust. Switching to triple-screened, low-dust bedding is one of the highest-impact respiratory interventions available to a trainer.

Every batch passes through three sequential mechanical screens before packaging:

  1. Pass 1 — Coarse Screen: Removes large debris, bark fragments, and oversize material.
  2. Pass 2 — Medium Screen: Removes mid-range particles including fine bark and cellulose fragments that become respirable when disturbed.
  3. Pass 3 — Fine Screen: The critical stage — removes PM10 and smaller particles. This is the pass most bedding manufacturers skip entirely.

The result is a finished product with a particle size distribution that consistently tests below respiratory hazard thresholds.

Standard unscreened shavings can carry 10–40× more respirable dust than our triple-screened product. Straw is among the worst options for IAD, harboring mold spores and organic particulates that trigger airway inflammation. Even "dust-extracted" shavings sold by most suppliers typically undergo only a single screening pass — removing coarse material while leaving the fine fraction that causes respiratory harm. Our three-pass process targets all three particle size categories, achieving dust levels comparable to laboratory-grade bedding at commercial pricing.

Most barns report visible improvement within the first two weeks. Common observations include reduced nasal discharge, less coughing on grooming and tack-up, improved coat quality, and horses that settle more calmly in the stall. For operations that track it, improved mucus scores at veterinary exam and reduced bedding costs (due to the pine pellet 4× expansion ratio) are the metrics trainers cite most often. Horses at elite performance levels — where aerobic capacity is measured in margins — tend to show the clearest measurable improvement.

Products & Specifications

We offer three triple-screened pine bedding products:

  • Pine Pellets (40 lb bags): Compressed pine pellets that expand 4× when wet. Extremely absorbent, lower volume needed per stall, easy stall pick. Preferred for barns with rubber mats. Most cost-effective per stall per month.
  • Micro Flake Shavings (3.5 cu ft bags): Fine-texture shavings for horses that prefer a softer bed. Ideal for sensitive horses or those recovering from injury.
  • Macro Flake Shavings (8.0 cu ft bags): Larger flake for deep-bedding systems or horses that prefer more cushion. Excellent drainage and airflow. Popular with deep litter operations.

Typical usage rates per stall per week:

  • Pine Pellets: 1–2 bags (40–80 lbs) for initial setup; 0.5–1 bag per week maintenance. The 4× expansion means a little goes a long way.
  • Micro Flake Shavings: 3–4 bags (10.5–14 cu ft) for initial bed; 1–2 bags per week maintenance.
  • Macro Flake Shavings: 2–3 bags (16–24 cu ft) for initial bed; 1 bag per week maintenance.
